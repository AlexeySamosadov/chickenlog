Know your birds

Add each hen and rooster with a name, breed, and optional hatch date or acquired date, and mark birds active or lost as your flock changes through the seasons. It's the simple roster every keeper means to write down and never does — except here it takes ten seconds per bird and never gets lost.

A health & treatment log you'll actually keep up

The hardest part of flock keeping is remembering what you did and when. ChickenLog gives you a dated health log for the whole flock or a single bird, covering the records that matter most:

  • Mites & lice — note the product and date so you can time the all-important second dose.
  • Worming — keep a worming schedule and avoid double-dosing or forgetting a season.
  • Illness, vaccines and vet visits — a clear history to show a vet or fellow keeper.
  • Coop notes — predator events, feed changes and coop maintenance, all dated.

Treatment timing without the guesswork

Most poultry mite and lice products need a repeat treatment after roughly a week to catch newly hatched eggs, and wormers are usually rotated on a schedule. A dated record turns "I think I did that last month" into a firm date you can plan the next dose from — which is exactly how you actually break a mite cycle instead of fighting it all summer.

Private, offline, yours

Everything is stored locally in your browser, so the app works out at the coop with no signal and nothing ever leaves your device. Unlimited birds, health records and notes are free forever; Pro unlocks CSV export, monthly egg charts and multi-coop for keepers running more than one flock.
