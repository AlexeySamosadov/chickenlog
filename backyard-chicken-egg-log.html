Why log your eggs at all?

Most backyard keepers track eggs on a paper calendar or a kitchen whiteboard, then lose it. But your egg count is the single best signal of flock health. A sudden drop can mean a hen is broody, molting, short on daylight, stressed by a predator, dehydrated, or carrying mites and lice. When you have a real daily record, you spot that dip in days instead of weeks — and you know whether it's the whole coop or just one bird going off lay.

What ChickenLog tracks

  • Daily egg counts — pick a date, tap +1/+6/+12, done in seconds.
  • Eggs today, this week, and last 30 days at a glance on the home screen.
  • Eggs per bird per week so you know your real production rate.
  • A 14-day laying trend chart so seasonal and stress dips jump out.
  • All-time totals — satisfying, and handy if you sell eggs.

Private by design

There's no sign-up and no cloud. Everything you log stays in your own browser using local storage, so it works in the coop with no signal and your data is yours alone. Pro adds CSV export and monthly production charts for keepers who want spreadsheets or sale records, but every keeper gets unlimited egg logging for free, forever.
